Column chromatography

The original language of this article is Chinese and parts of it have been translated using DeepL

 This experiment uses column chromatography to separate and purify compounds. Specifically, a glass tube is filled with a solid powder called silica gel and then a solvent is used to bring out the different compounds in the mixture and collect them in separate bottles.


 Column chromatography is a method of analytical chemistry that can be used to separate and purify the different compounds in a mixture. Specifically, the separation is achieved by using the difference in the interaction forces between the different compounds in the stationary phase (silica gel) and the mobile phase (solvent). In general, compounds with higher interaction forces are more likely to be adsorbed by the stationary phase and move more slowly; compounds with lower interaction forces are more likely to be carried away by the mobile phase and move more quickly. In this way, it is possible to separate the different compounds according to their speed of movement. I used an organic acid called benzoic acid and an organic alcohol called benzyl alcohol as a mixture, and they could be separated by column chromatography.


 To do the experiment, I first measured 10 ml of benzoic acid and 10 ml of benzyl alcohol in a measuring cylinder and then mixed them together to get a mixture. Next, I filtered the silica gel through a funnel and filter paper to remove some impurities. I then filled a glass tube with silica gel using a glass rod to form a homogeneous columnar layer. Then, using a syringe, I injected the mixture into the top of the glass tube and let it slowly penetrate into the silica gel layer. I then used a solvent pump to inject the solvent from the bottom of the glass tube and let it move up the silicone layer, driving the different compounds in the mixture different distances. I used a collection rack to place different bottles at the exit of the glass tube and collected the different liquids at time intervals.


 To observe the results, I first tested the acidity and alkalinity of the liquids in the different bottles separately using an acid-base test strip and found that one bottle was acidic, one bottle was neutral and one bottle was alkaline. I deduced that the acidic liquid was benzoic acid, the neutral liquid was benzyl alcohol and the alkaline liquid was a solvent.

 To test my deduction, I then used a reagent called iron trichloride to drop into each of the different bottles of liquid and found that only the acidic liquid turned red, indicating the presence of carboxyl groups. This proved my deduction to be correct and I successfully separated and purified benzoic acid and benzyl alcohol by column chromatography.
